Idea Bank

The Grand Challenges began by listening to hundreds of School of Education Faculty and Staff about the most promising areas of interdisciplinary change. Here's what they had to say:

What if:

We could create new approaches to prepare young children born into poverty for success in school and in life.

What if:

We could collaborate with health care researchers to use neuroimaging to measure how learners experiment with new ideas.

What if:

We could develop promising approaches for using the arts to improve issues of student identity and health.

What if:

We could develop new programs that address the academic, social, financial, physical, mental and emotional barriers that keep students from graduating high school and from preparing for career opportunities.

What if:

We could mitigate the effects of trauma on the health, expression and educational horizons of disenfranchised underemployed adults.

What if:

We could use creative expression through the arts to better health and learning opportunities that improve engagement among K-12 students — in and out of schools.

What if:

We could refine our ability to make causal inferences from experimental and quasi-experimental research designs that result in improved abilities to measure outcomes and practices.

What if:

We could use data analytics and machine learning methods to understand how learners engage in complex domains and anticipate learning outcomes.

What if:

We could work with rural school districts and communities to conduct research and develop new initiatives that address labor shortages, access to high quality programs, arts collaboratives, and/or mental health care issues.

What if:

We could develop new approaches that provide at risk and incarcerated youth with new starts toward building fulfilling lives.

What if:

We could develop a new discourse around critical perspectives that can critique and inform policy initiatives.

How will the grant program work?

UW SOE Grand Challenges initiative will ignite the imagination of our faculty and staff to explore innovative ideas that define new frontiers in education, health and creative expression. The goals of the SOE Grand Challenges are to:

  • Spark and support innovative ideas, practices and programs across the SOE community.
  • Build a culture of interdisciplinary innovation across SOE.
  • Support projects that will make a profound difference in the lives of our  students, community, state and our world.

There are two kinds of Grand Challenges Projects: Connect Projects eligible for up to $25,000 in support; and Transform Projects eligible for up to $250,000.

Connect

Across Disciplines

  • Feb-Sep 2017

    The Connect phase will inspire and facilitate interactions among UW School of Education faculty staff to build interdisciplinary teams that will identify and design solutions for critical social and scholarly problems.

    The goal of Connect is to build a portfolio of exciting new projects and programs that will be competitive for private and public funding support. Connect projects will live on the Grand Challenges website and will serve as a showcase for the best ideas we have to offer as an SOE community.

  • Feb-Sep 2017

    Connect aims to help teams articulate ideas through a multimedia Connect Project, acquire team members and community partners, meet with relevant external funding sources and explore appropriate public and private funding opportunities.

    Connect starts with a consultation with the Network team to discuss your ideas, round out your team, and discuss potential measures of impact. The Consultation will allow the Network team to understand your needs as a team moving forward, and to help guide the process.

  • Feb-Sep 2017

    Connect projects may apply to participate in a competition for up to $25,000 in a Fall Connect Project Competition.

    Small discretionary funds (up to $1000) will be available to participants to assist with this startup phase.

Engage

With the Challenge

  • Engage will invite School of Education teams to develop larger scale projects and enter into a competition for up to $250,000 to spark an ambitious agenda for inquiry, impact, and innovation.

Transform

The world with your ideas

  • Transform is a School of Education Gala that will invite community members, faculty, staff and friends from across the country to pick from the best ideas and projects to support 2-3 projects for more extensive funding.

How Will The Grand Challenges Move Forward?

Who can participate in the Grand Challenges?

Each Grand Challenge team must include at least two UW-Madison School of Education Faculty or Staff from different departments. Teams will represent multiple academic disciplines, and may also include team members from across UW-Madison, other universities, and the wider community.

Proposals that aim at community impact should include relevant community partners; proposals that include innovative collaboration should include potential collaborators.

Each team will designate a primary investigator (PI) who will serve as the contact for the proposal. Faculty and staff members can serve on multiple Grand Challenge teams, but can only serve as the PI on one proposal. Only UW School of Education Faculty and Staff can serve as PIs on any proposal.

What are the criteria for a strong proposal?

Grand Challenge Projects are collaboratively-written proposal that address three key concepts: Inquiry, Innovation and Impact.

Inquiry — What is your burning question? How will the work extend and apply what we know and can do in key areas of expression, education or health-related scholarship? How will it transform the field?

Innovation — How will the proposal break new ground? How will the work use interdisciplinarity to cross boundaries into new programmatic, conceptual or aesthetic areas?

Impact — What measurable and authentic difference will the work make in the lives of people in our community, our state, our country, or our world? How can this difference be measured? Why is this the right time to address this problem?

What is the difference between the Connect and Transform Competition?

Because the Grand Challenges initiative intends to inspire all different types of projects with differing scope and magnitude, participants can choose to enter their project to compete for one of two levels of awards:

Connect Grants — Multiple grants (~6-8) in the amount of ~$25,000 will be awarded to teams who complete a Connect Projects. Connect Awards will be made at the beginning of the 2017-18 Academic Year. Proposals submitted for Connect Projects cannot be considered for the Transform Competition.

Transform Grants — A smaller number (3-5) of grants of up to $250,000 will be awarded to teams who complete an Transform Project. Transform Projects will be selected through the process outlined in the Criteria for Review of Grand Challenges Projects Competing for Funding. Transform Projects will require an additional step in the review process that involves an SOE wide public event with a panel of judges and public input.

Who will coordinate the Grand Challenges?

The Grand Challenges will be coordinated by the Wisconsin Collaborative Education Research Network (the Network) in partnership with leaders from across the School of Education. The Network’s role is to inspire faculty and staff to seek out the kinds of support they need to make a difference in the worlds of scholarship, creativity and everyday life.

How will all Grand Challenge Projects be supported?

Each Grand Challenge Project, regardless of the level of funding for which they choose to apply, will get a web page that highlights (revised, if necessary) posters, emphasizes key issues raised in the proposal, and provides links to relevant current and past work of the team members. This web-page, housed on the Grand Challenges site, will serve as the primary information source and point-of-contact for community members interested in knowing more about and supporting Grand Challenges projects.
